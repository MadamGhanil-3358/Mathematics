Whole Numbers & Place Value 📊

Definition

Numbers are symbols used for counting and measuring. In Form One, we study Natural Numbers (1,2,3,...) and Whole Numbers (0,1,2,3,...). Place value helps us read large numbers: ones, tens, hundreds, thousands.

🔢 Example: 5,267 = 5 thousands + 2 hundreds + 6 tens + 7 ones

Real-life example

In Tanzania, you might count shillings: 15,500 TSh means 1 ten-thousand, 5 thousands, and 5 hundreds.

  • ✅ 2,450 → two thousand four hundred fifty
  • ✅ 30,000 → thirty thousand

Fractions & Decimals 🥧

What is a fraction?

A fraction represents a part of a whole. Written as \(\frac{a}{b}\) where a = numerator, b = denominator (b ≠ 0). For example, \(\frac{3}{4}\) means 3 parts out of 4 equal parts.

🍕 If you eat 2 slices of pizza cut into 8 slices → fraction = 2/8 = 1/4.

Everyday Maths

When sharing maize or beans: 1/2 kg, 3/4 litre. Also decimals: 0.5 = 1/2, 0.75 = 3/4.

  • Convert 0.6 to fraction: 6/10 = 3/5.

Intro to Algebra 🔤

Algebraic Expressions

Algebra uses letters (variables) to represent unknown numbers. Example: \(x + 5 = 12\). We solve to find \(x = 7\). Simple like balancing a scale!

✨ Key rule: same operation on both sides keeps equation true.

Real Application

If a book costs 2,500 TSh and you have ‘b’ shillings, the change = b - 2500. Also, “three times a number plus 4” is written as \(3n+4\).

Basic Geometry 📐

Shapes & Angles

Geometry studies points, lines, angles, and shapes. A line has no endpoints; a ray starts from a point. Common shapes: square, rectangle, triangle, circle.

🔺 Triangle has 3 sides, sum of interior angles = 180°. 🔲 Square has 4 equal sides and 4 right angles.

Construction in daily life

Roof of houses forms triangles (strong structure). Tiles often have rectangular or square patterns.

  • Area of rectangle = length × width.
  • Perimeter = 2×(l+w).

Introduction to Statistics 📈

Data & graphs

Statistics means collecting, organizing, and interpreting data. Form One learns about pictographs, bar charts, mean, median, and mode.

📊 Mean = sum of all data values divided by number of values.

Tanzania school example

Suppose test scores: 70, 85, 90, 75, 80. Mean = (70+85+90+75+80)/5 = 400/5 = 80. The most common score is Mode.

  • Bar charts help compare harvest yields across regions.
